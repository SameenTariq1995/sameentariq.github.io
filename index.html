About Me

I am a behavioural and experimental economist whose research studies the impact of behavioural interventions on education outcomes and economic decision-making using field and laboratory experiments. My work examines how context-specific behavioural tools—such as nudges, framing, and psychological training—can shape risk preferences and educational outcomes.

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a Short-Term Consultant with the World Bank (SARCE and Sustainable Development teams), a Senior Research Assistant at the Centre for Economic Research in Pakistan (CERP), and a Teaching Fellow at Information Technology University (ITU). I currently serve as an Associate Teacher in Economics at the University of East Anglia.
